Plant Cell Orgenelles & Functions

Cell Wall

Cell Wall

The cell wall is an extra layer of protection providing structural support from damage.

Cytoplasm

Cytoplasm

A substance that fills the cell, allowing the cell to retain intensity and shape. It also allows movement of other organelles.

Vacuole

Vacuole

Vacuoles are a gel-like sack storing waste, water, etc. They also help to support the cell by exerting pressure, called turgor pressure.

Cell Membrane

Cell Membrane

The Cell Membrane is a semipermeable membrane that allows and denies entry, and exit of packages.

Chloroplast

Chloroplast

Chloroplast is responsible for photosynthesis, allowing sunlight to enter the cell and be exported.

6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Smooth E.R.

Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum

The smooth endoplasmic reticulum is like a refinery, detoxifying organelles.

Rough E.R.

Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum

The rough endoplasmic reticulum is a gel-like body containing ribosome moles, it manufactures and distributes several proteins.

Golgi Body

Golgi Body

The Golgi Apparatus is like amazon, it packages and ships proteins & fats.

Lysosomes

Lysosomes

Lysosomes break down cellular waste into more simple compounds. Lysosomes also send these compounds to the cytoplasm as building materials.

Mitochondria

Mitochondria

Mitochondria are the powerhouse of the cell, converting oxygen & nutrients into energy, more scientifically known as Adenosine 5'-triphosphate, or ATP for short.

Nucleus

Nucleus

The Nucleus stores Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA) and coordinates the cell's events.

Nucleolus

Nucleolus

The nucleolus is responsible for facilitating ribosome biogenesis.

Ribosomes

Ribosomes

Ribosomes are proteins that are composed of 60% RNA, and 40% protein.

   

Bacteria Cell Structure & Functions

Cell Wall

Cell Wall

The cell wall is an extra layer of protection providing structural support from damage.

Cell Membrane

Cell Membrane

The Cell Membrane is a semipermeable membrane that allows and denies entry and exit of packages.

Ribosomes

Ribosomes

Ribosomes are protein organelles that are composed of 60% RNA, and 40% protein.

Cytoplasm

Cytoplasm

A substance that fills the cell, allowing the cell to retain intensity and shape. It also allows movement of other organelles.

Pilli

Pilli

Pilli is a string-like substance that allows the bacterial cell to stick to other surfaces.

Flagellum

Flagellum

Flagellum is the cell's tail, it allows the cell to move around inside it's host.

Plasmids

Plasmids

Plasmids are small, double-stranded circular or linear DNA strands that can exist outside the nucleoid.

Plasma Membrane

Plasma Membrane

Another wall that encloses the cytoplasm composed mainly of water, and also contains enzymes. Without this membrane, the entire contents of the cell would spill out. No Membrane, No Cell

Nucleoid

Nucleoid

The nucleoid is a region that holds chromosomes.

Capsule

Capsule

The capsule is another layer of protection that surrounds the cell wall. It helps protect against phagocytosis and helps the bacterial cell adhere to surfaces.
